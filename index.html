üìö Time Management

Master your schedule by applying the Eisenhower Matrix, a productivity tool for prioritizing tasks effectively:

QuadrantDescriptionExamples
Q1: Urgent & ImportantDo these tasks immediately.Assignments due tomorrow, an exam today, urgent meetings
Q2: Not Urgent but ImportantPlan and schedule for long-term success.Course revisions, skill-building, exercise, setting goals
Q3: Urgent but Not ImportantDelegate or minimize disruptions.Unnecessary emails, social requests, notifications
Q4: Not Urgent & Not ImportantAvoid these time-wasters.Binge-watching, excessive gaming, endless scrolling
Tip: Combine this strategy with digital planners like Notion, Trello, or Google Calendar to track tasks visually.

üß† Memorization Techniques

Enhance memory with scientifically backed methods:

  • Spaced Repetition: Reinforce knowledge over increasing intervals. Try apps like Anki or RemNote.
  • Chunking: Break down information into digestible pieces. Example: group digits like 123-456-789.
  • Mnemonics: Create acronyms or rhymes. Example: "HOMES" for Great Lakes (Huron, Ontario, Michigan, Erie, Superior).
  • Visualization: Use images or diagrams to make abstract concepts tangible.
  • Memory Palace: Visualize placing information in different locations of a familiar space.
  • Active Recall: Frequently quiz yourself instead of re-reading.
  • Interleaving: Mix subjects or topics to improve retention and understanding.
Top Combo: Spaced Repetition + Active Recall = Long-Term Learning Superpower
üéì Study & Exam Tips

Before the Exam:

  • Design a study plan with clear milestones
  • Use the 5-minute rule to start tasks and break procrastination
  • Practice using past exams and quizzes
  • Ensure balanced nutrition, hydration, and 7-8 hours of sleep

During the Exam:

  • Skim through the whole paper first
  • Start with the easiest questions to build momentum
  • Allocate time for each section
  • Mark tricky questions and return later

General Tips:

  • Use the Pomodoro Technique: 25 minutes work + 5 minutes break
  • Form study groups to share insights and test each other
  • Summarize topics in your own words or teach someone else
